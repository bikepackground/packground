# ALTAY QUEST: BIKEPACKING IN THE BORDERLAND OF MONGOLIA AND RUSSIA

**Last updated:** September 2017  
*Please help keep this information up to date!*

## OVERVIEW

The Altay is situated at the junction of four countries: Mongolia, China, Russia, and Kazakhstan. Visiting requires special permits applied for two months in advance; in 2014, a mandatory guide rule was introduced for the Tavan Bogd National Park. 

To avoid this bureaucracy, we created the **Altay Quest**: a route linking the Mongolian and Russian Altay by excluding Tavan Bogd. It crosses two national parks and a UNESCO site, following ancient migration paths of the Khoton nomads and leading to the mystical Ukok Plateau. It is a demanding journey with steep gradients, neglected double tracks, and two trailless hike-a-bike passes.

## SUGGESTED ROUTE

The route consists of two loops and a one-way section, connected by the border crossing at Tashanta:

1. **Arrival:** Via Barnaul, Russia (train/plane), then bus to Gorno-Altaysk. Cycle the Chuiski Trakt or take the connection via Teletskoye Lake and the Chulyshman Valley to Kosh-Agach.
2. **Ukok Loop (Russia):** Collect border permits in Kosh-Agach. After the loop, cross the border to Bayan-Ölgii.
3. **Altay Loop (Mongolia):** From Ölgii through the Bulgan Valley to Khovd, then traverse the Tsambagaraav National Park before returning to Ölgii.
4. **Grand Finale (Ölgii to Ulaangom):** Through the rugged Kharkhiraa Mountains. Note: The direct connection from Ulaangom to the Russian border is currently impassable due to missing bridges.

<details>
<summary>UKOK LOOP (RUSSIA) – DETAILS</summary>
<p>
**Route:** Starts with a climb over the "old" Tiopla Kljutsch Pass to the Ukok Plateau. Features a labyrinth of rivers against the Tavan Bogd massif. Two steep passes and a descent lead into the Argut Valley and the village of Beljashi (Djazator).

**Argut Valley Extension:** A rough track follows the river to the settlement of Argut. Warning: The trekking paths over high passes to Tyungur and Mt. Belukha are impassable by bike. The Karagemski Pass back to Kosh-Agach requires pack horses rented in advance from the Tour Baza in Beljashi.

**Logistics:** Mandatory border permit (Propusk) required (2 months in advance). Three military checkpoints. Carry 5 days of food.
</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary>ALTAY LOOP (MONGOLIA) – DETAILS</summary>
<p>
**Route:** Arid Bulgan Valley over several passes to Khovd. After Tolbo, it becomes a dirt track. From Khovd, follow the sandy "washboard" road to Khongor. Here, a faint trail climbs 1,000m elevation gain over 6km—significant pushing guaranteed.

**Tsambagaraav Massif:** Requires numerous fords across glacial streams (keep shoes on for grip). After a grueling climb, enter a high valley of yurts beneath the glaciated summit. 

**Shortcut:** Turn east at Tolbo to reach Khongor directly via the main road. No special permits required.
</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary>KHARKHIRAA MOUNTAINS (MONGOLIA) – DETAILS</summary>
<p>
**Route:** From Ölgii through Hovd Canyon to Archit Nuur. Section is heavily sandy until the ascent to Khar Davaa. Leads through high valleys of Khoton nomads.

**The Kharkhiraa Pass:** Reaches 2,900m between glaciated peaks. The final 7km are trailless and physically exhausting. Warning: The path through the gorge toward Ulaangom is often blocked by high water. If impassable, a detour is required: a steep, pathless climb to a high plateau connecting near the Khar Tarvagatain Uurkhai coal mine.

**Logistics:** Full services in Ölgii and Ulaangom. Carry 5 days of food.
</p>
</details>

## INFRASTRUCTURE & LOGISTICS

* **Water:** Sources marked in GPX. In Mongolia, all water should be treated. In Russia, quality is high but treatment recommended.
* **Timing:** Mid-August to late September. Avoids summer mosquitoes and early October snow.
* **Navigation:** Many tracks not on OpenStreetMap. Use the **Soviet Military Maps app** with our GPX data.
* **Difficulty:** High fitness and mental perseverance required. Light bikepacking setup is essential.
