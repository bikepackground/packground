# PUNA LOOP: BIKEPACKING IN NORTHWEST ARGENTINA

**Last updated:** December 2015

## OVERVIEW

A gargantuan landscape of stone, sand, and dust; a world of light and shadow in shades of grey and brown. The Puna, a high-altitude mountain desert in the heart of the Andes, serves as the stage for this extraordinary route. 

Here, salt lakes stretch endlessly toward the horizon and volcanic cones cast long shadows over the desert floor. In the absolute silence of the night, when the omnipresent wind finally rests, you are left with nothing but sparkling stars and profound solitude. This loop showcases the most beautiful and raw parts of Northwest Argentina.

## ROUTE & TERRAIN

**Acclimatization:** The journey begins at El Eje on the legendary Ruta 40. A narrow paved road climbs to Antofagasta de la Sierra (3,400 m), the only town on the route with tourist infrastructure. Use this as your base for acclimatization and final supplies.

**Into the No-Man's Land:** Beyond the asphalt, the Puna reveals its true face. Via sandy dirt roads and faint 4x4 tracks, you will conquer passes up to 4,700 m. You will cross the white expanses of the Salar de Antofalla and Salar de Arizaro and ride through the massive caldera of the Galan Volcano. A major highlight is camping at Laguna Grande, a turquoise haven and a primary breeding ground for Andean flamingos.

**The Descent:** From the hamlet of El Peñón, you traverse the Campo de Piedra Pómez—a surreal field of wind-sculpted pumice—before descending the sandy Paso de Buenaventura. This section follows an improved mule path down to the lowlands, concluding in Fiambalá. 
*Note: Due to altitude and the steep nature of the final descent, we strongly recommend riding this route in the suggested direction.*

## INFRASTRUCTURE & LOGISTICS

**Supplies:** We completed the route in 17 riding days. Aside from Antofagasta de la Sierra, Tolar Grande, and El Peñón, there are no resupply points. You must be prepared to carry 6–7 days of food at a time.

**Water:** Sources are generally no more than 24 hours apart (see waypoints), but they require filtration and meticulous planning.

**Safety:** Given the extreme remoteness, we recommend that solo bikepackers carry a satellite communication device.

## DIFFICULTY

Beyond technical riding skills, this route demands high physical fitness and mental resilience.

**Environment:** The Puna is defined by relentless, gale-force winds from the northwest. You must be able to endure daily storms, high altitudes, and absolute isolation. 

**Requirements:** This loop is reserved for experienced bikepackers capable of planning and riding under extreme conditions. It is a world-class challenge where the bike is often the only companion for days.
