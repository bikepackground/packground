# ILLAMPU CIRCUIT: BIKEPACKING VERSION

**Last updated:** August 2019

## OVERVIEW

The Illampu Circuit is a premier trekking route at the northern tip of Bolivia’s Cordillera Real. It circumnavigates the mighty Illampu—the fourth-highest peak in the range at 6,368 meters—crossing several high-altitude passes. While the region remains wild and rarely visited, we were inspired by the routes of Cass Gilbert and Michael Dammer (https://bikepacking.com) to explore the area further. 

Satellite imagery revealed jeep tracks over the two primary passes—Abra de la Calzada (5,090 m) and Sarani Pass—allowing us to expand the traditional trek into a full bikepacking loop via Cooco, Ancoma, and Sorata. What resulted is one of the most spectacular, high-glacier tracks in the Andes.

## ROUTE & TERRAIN

**Acclimatization:** Do not underestimate the elevation. Before starting, spend several days acclimatizing in La Paz or the more relaxed Copacabana on Lake Titicaca. A warm-up ride from Copacabana to Achacachi on dirt roads is highly recommended.

**Starting Point:** Starting in Achacachi, a bustling town at the foothills of the Cordillera (accessible by bus from La Paz), you immediately face a significant climb. The circuit is best tackled counter-clockwise.

**The Mama Coca Connection:** For a longer adventure, combine this with the Mama Coca Route https://bikepacking.com/routes/mama-coca/ from La Paz. Note that the section between Laguna Q’ara Kota and Laguna Cacha involves many trackless passages (expect 50% hike-a-bike); a detour through the valley via San Juan de Chachacomani is a sensible alternative.

**The Ride:** Expect rough dirt tracks. Due to the extreme altitude and steep gradients on the pass approaches, some pushing is inevitable. Lightweight equipment is essential to ensure the steep sections remain manageable and enjoyable.

## INFRASTRUCTURE & LOGISTICS

**Resupply:** Basic supplies are available in Achacachi, the mountain village of Cooco, and Sorata. For specialized items like energy bars or dried fruit, stock up in La Paz or Copacabana. While the shop in Cooco is often well-stocked with oats (avena), bread, and vegetables, availability can fluctuate.

**Accommodation:** Excellent wild camping can be found near Abra Calzada and in the valley beyond the Sarani Pass, away from the road. In the tiny settlements of Cooco or the nearly abandoned Ancoma, you can often ask permission to stay at the local school.

**Water:** Water is abundant throughout most of the route, though filtering is mandatory. Note that the stretch departing from Ancoma is significantly drier, so plan accordingly.

## DIFFICULTY

Despite the relatively short distance, the Illampu Circuit is a serious undertaking.

**Altitude:** You will be riding almost exclusively between 4,000 and 5,000 meters. Peak physical condition is mandatory.

**Conditions:** The four main passes feature washed-out tracks and steep climbs. Even in the dry season (late July), passes like Abra de la Calzada can be covered in snow.

**Requirements:** This route demands thorough preparation and a window of stable weather.
